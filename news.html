Malaysia — NAVIER YACHTS is proud to announce that two patrol boats built for the Malaysia Coast Guard have been officially commissioned into active service. This achievement represents a significant milestone in our partnership with Malaysian maritime security agencies and demonstrates our capability in delivering professional-grade patrol vessels.

Project Overview

The two patrol boats were built to exacting specifications for coastal patrol and rapid response operations in Malaysian waters. Each vessel incorporates advanced design features specifically tailored to the operational requirements of the Malaysia Coast Guard.

Vessel Capabilities:

  • High-speed patrol operations (40+ knots)
  • All-weather capability for year-round operations
  • Advanced navigation and communication systems
  • Radar and surveillance equipment
  • Boarding and interception capabilities
  • Long-range fuel capacity for extended patrols
  • Professional crew accommodation
  • Emergency response equipment

SnakeHead Series Proven Performance

These vessels are part of our SnakeHead patrol boat series, which has been specifically designed for professional maritime security applications. The aluminum construction provides excellent durability while maintaining high performance characteristics essential for patrol operations.

International Partnership Success

This project demonstrates NAVIER YACHTS' ability to work effectively with international government agencies, meeting strict specifications, delivery schedules, and compliance requirements. The successful commissioning validates our engineering approach and construction quality.

Commissioning Ceremony

The vessels were formally commissioned in an official ceremony attended by Malaysia Coast Guard leadership and government officials. Both boats have now entered active service and are deployed for coastal patrol duties.

Project Details

Client: Malaysia Coast Guard

Vessels: 2 × SnakeHead Patrol Boats

Commissioning Date: August 1, 2024
